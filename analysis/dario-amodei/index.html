Executive Summary

Dario Amodei is the co-founder and CEO of Anthropic, the AI safety company behind Claude. A physicist and biophysicist by training (PhD Princeton), he co-led development of GPT-2 and GPT-3 at OpenAI and co-invented RLHF before departing in 2020 over disagreements about safety prioritization. He describes himself as "an accidental CEO of an accidental business."

Under his leadership, Anthropic has grown from $0 to $19B+ annualized revenue, making it one of the fastest-growing companies in history. His dual mandate — building frontier AI while maintaining rigorous safety standards — creates a productive tension that defines both the company and his leadership. He has pledged to donate 80% of his wealth and maintains two firm "red lines": no mass surveillance, no fully autonomous weapons.

His thinking is worth studying because he operates at the intersection of frontier capability and safety restraint. Where most AI leaders lean optimistic or pessimistic, Amodei holds both simultaneously — authoring "Machines of Loving Grace" (an optimistic vision of AI compressing 50–100 years of progress into 5–10) while also publishing "The Adolescence of Technology" warning of existential risks. This duality makes his strategic thinking unusually nuanced.
